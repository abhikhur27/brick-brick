Strategy
Pricing Tips for Pre-Revenue Conversations

Use these before and during any sales call. These are your mental frameworks, not scripts.

01

Lead with a Founder's Rate

Offer your first 2–3 clients a 20–30% discount. Frame it as early access pricing — not desperation. Get testimonials and case studies in return.

"We're offering early access pricing for our first few clients this quarter."
02

Always Anchor High First

Quote the Growth tier first, then offer Starter as the step-down. Never lead with the cheapest option — it sets the wrong ceiling.

"Most clients in your situation start at the Growth tier. We also have a Starter option if you want to begin smaller."
03

Bundle the Retainer Into the Sale

Normalize recurring revenue from day one. The client should expect ongoing fees before they sign, not after.

"The build is $4,500 and we include the first month of maintenance — then it's $400/mo to keep it running and improving."
04

Charge a Discovery Fee

For larger projects, charge $300–$500 just to scope the work. It filters unserious clients and pays you to write the proposal. Apply it toward the build if they sign.

"To map this properly, we do a paid scoping session. That fee goes toward your build if you move forward."
05

Compete on ROI, Not Price

Don't race SMBs to the bottom. Frame every proposal around what the automation saves or earns them — not what it costs you to build.

"Most clients save 10–20 hours a week from this. At $30/hr that's $1,200–$2,400/mo back in their business."

💡 Why These Numbers Work

At $50/hr, a 40-hour build = $2,000. These prices give you healthy margin and reflect the value delivered, not just hours worked. Even small businesses save 10–20 hrs/week from automation — that's worth $500–$2,000/mo to them. You're not selling labor. You're selling reclaimed time and reduced errors.
